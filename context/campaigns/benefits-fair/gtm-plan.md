## Executive Summary

This is a high-intensity, 12-week campaign (June 1 - August 22, 2026) to capture virtual benefits fair deals before the September buying window closes. The campaign combines ABM for high-value targets with scalable outbound for the broader market, leveraging a broker channel strategy as a force multiplier.

**The opportunity:**
- Blue ocean market with near-zero competition (Ahrefs KD = 0, only 2 small direct competitors)
- vFairs already has 72 customers and $3.08M in benefits fair revenue
- 1,110 warm contacts in HubSpot
- Buying window opens NOW (June-August), events run September-November
- Insurance broker channel can multiply deals 5-20x per broker relationship

**Revenue target:** 15-20 new benefits fair deals in the 2026 enrollment season, representing $200K-$400K in new revenue.

## 1. Strategic Decision: ABM + Outbound Hybrid (Not Either/Or)

### Recommendation: Run BOTH in parallel, with different audiences

**ABM (Account-Based Marketing)** for:
- The 46 open opportunities + 20 SQLs already in HubSpot pipeline
- Insurance broker channel partners (Gallagher, Lockton, Brown & Brown, Baldwin Group, Alliant, HUB, Woodruff Sawyer, USI)
- Fortune 500 / Forbes 2000 companies with 5,000+ employees
- Existing vFairs customers who use us for other event types but not benefits fairs (cross-sell)

**Scalable Outbound** for:
- Lookalike audience of Benefits Managers / HR Directors at 1,000-10,000 employee US companies
- Insurance brokerages not yet in our pipeline
- Government agencies and universities (seasonal buyers)

### Why hybrid, not pure ABM:
- Pure ABM limits scale. With only 66 identified high-value accounts, ABM alone won't hit the revenue target.
- The benefits fair buyer is seasonal and urgent -- they're actively searching right now. Outbound catches the "just starting to research" buyers that ABM misses.
- The broker channel is inherently scalable (one deal = many clients) but requires ABM-style relationship building.

## 4. Lookalike Audience Building (Clay Workflow)

### Step-by-Step Clay Process

**1. Seed list (existing customers):**
Upload 72 benefits fair customers with: company name, industry, employee count, revenue, location

**2. Company lookalike search:**
- Clay "Find Similar Companies" using top 20 customers as seed
- Filter: US-based, 1,000-50,000 employees, target industries
- Additional signals:
  - Recent "Benefits Manager" job postings (People Data Labs or LinkedIn Jobs API)
  - 3+ office locations (multi-location signal via Apollo or Clearbit)
  - Companies using Workday, ADP, or PlanSource (technographic signal via BuiltWith or HG Insights)

**3. Contact enrichment per company:**
- Find 2-3 contacts per company: Benefits Manager/Director + HR Director/VP
- Enrich with: email (Apollo/Hunter), LinkedIn URL, phone
- Clay waterfall: Apollo first, then Hunter.io, then Clearbit

**4. Deduplication:**
- Cross-reference against HubSpot database (1,110 existing contacts)
- Remove any existing customers or active opportunities

**5. Scoring:**
Score each prospect 1-100 based on:
- Employee count (5,000+ = 30pts, 1,000-5,000 = 20pts)
- Multi-location (3+ offices = 15pts)
- Target industry (Healthcare/Insurance/Gov/Edu = 15pts)
- Benefits Manager title confirmed (10pts)
- Recent benefits job posting (10pts)
- Fortune 500/Forbes 2000 (10pts)
- Distributed workforce signal (remote job postings = 10pts)

**6. Segmentation:**
- Score 70+: ABM treatment (personalized outreach, LinkedIn Ads matched audience)
- Score 40-69: Outbound email sequence via Smartlead
- Score below 40: Add to nurture list only

**Expected output:** 500-800 target companies, 1,000-1,500 contacts

## 5. Smartlead Execution Plan

### Inbox Setup
- 3-4 warmed inboxes (already available per Aatir)
- Domain: use a subdomain or allied domain (e.g., team@vfairs-events.com) to protect main domain reputation
- Warmup: ensure 30+ days of warmup complete before sending
- Daily send limit: 25-30 per inbox to maintain deliverability

### Campaign Config
- Send window: 8:00 AM - 11:00 AM recipient local time (Tue-Thu)
- Sequence gap: 3-day minimum between touches
- Auto-stop on reply (any reply)
- Unsubscribe link in every email (CAN-SPAM compliance)

### Tracking
- Custom tracking domain
- Open tracking: ON (for sequence logic)
- Click tracking: ON (for engagement scoring)
- Push positive replies to HubSpot via webhook or Zapier

### Follow-up Process
- Positive replies routed to AE within 2 hours
- AE books demo within 48 hours of positive reply
- Meeting booked triggers HubSpot deal creation at "Demo Scheduled" stage
