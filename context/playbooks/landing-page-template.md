# vFairs Feature Landing Page Template

> Reference template for the `/write-landing-page` skill and any human copywriter working on vFairs.com pages.
> For deep writing rules and examples, see `.claude/skills/page-builder/writing-guide.md`.

---

## Page Type Guide

| Page Type | Reader's Real Question | H1 Focus |
|---|---|---|
| **Feature** | "Does this specific thing work the way I need it to?" | The output of using the feature |
| **Product** | "Does this product cover everything I need?" | What organizers/attendees can do, not what the product is |
| **Solution** | "Can vFairs handle my specific event type end-to-end?" | The event outcome, not the platform name |
| **Industry** | "Does vFairs understand my world?" | Vocabulary this vertical uses for their own problems |

---

## The 10-Fold Architecture

```
[1]  HERO              Eyebrow + value prop H1 + subheading + CTA
[2]  TRUST STRIP       Logos + awards — no copy, instant credibility
[3]  FEATURES INTRO    Centered heading + subheading (bridges trust → features)
[4]  FEATURE BLOCKS    4–8 two-column sections (the page's core weight)
[5]  SOCIAL PROOF      Real quote + stat — re-establishes trust mid-scroll
[6]  USE CASE GRID     Show the range (solution + industry pages only)
[7]  PRODUCT TOUR      See-it-in-action moment (product + industry pages)
[8]  RELATED CONTENT   One case study or guide — SEO + nurture value
[9]  FAQ               Handle the real objections before they bounce
[10] CLOSING CTA       Restate the outcome. Remove the risk. Ask again.
```

---

## FOLD 1 — HERO

### What it needs to do
Get the right reader to stay. Get the wrong reader to leave. Do both in under 5 seconds.

### Structure

```
EYEBROW (mandatory): [Category keyword — plain, no punctuation]
    This is where the primary SEO keyword lives.
    Examples: "Speaker Management Software" / "Event Registration" / "Badge Printing"
    Rule: Plain text label. No verbs. No exclamation. Just the category name.

H1: [First-order benefit or value proposition] — max 10 words
    Rule: Names what the reader GETS or STOPS suffering — not what the product IS
    Test: "So what?" — if the answer adds meaning, the H1 failed
    The keyword from the eyebrow must NOT be repeated verbatim as the H1

Subheading: [2–3 specific capabilities that deliver the H1 promise] — max 30 words
    Rule: Concrete nouns and actions only. No "transform," "empower," "elevate," "seamless"
    Format: "[Capability 1], [capability 2], and [outcome] — all in one platform."

Primary CTA:   See [Feature] in Action  OR  Book a Demo
Secondary CTA: Watch 2-Min Overview     OR  See Pricing
    Rule: Never "Get Started," "Learn More," "Submit"
```

### H1 Angles — pick the one that fits the page's core story

| Angle | Pattern | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Pain removal | Stop [doing painful thing] | "Stop Managing Speakers Across Spreadsheets and Email Threads" |
| Platform advantage | [Outcome] without [switching/friction] | "Run the Entire Speaker Lifecycle Without Switching Tools" |
| Outcome-forward | Get [specific result] without [specific effort] | "Get Every Speaker Profiled, Scheduled, and Ready Without Chasing Anyone" |

When offering H1 options, always give one of each angle so the choice is deliberate.

### What NOT to do

| Wrong | Why | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| "Speaker Management Software" as H1 | Category label, not a value prop | Move to eyebrow. Write a benefit as H1. |
| "Simplify Your Speaker Management" | Vague action — simplify how? | Name the specific outcome or pain removed |
| "The Ultimate Speaker Management Solution" | "Ultimate" / "solution" = zero signal | Name what it actually does |

## FOLD 2 — TRUST STRIP

### What it needs to do
Give the reader permission to keep reading. Credibility in one horizontal row.

### Structure
```
[6–8 customer logos — recognizable names relevant to this page's ICP]
[G2 Leader badge] [Gartner Magic Quadrant badge] [Capterra badge]
```

### Rules
- For industry pages: weight logos toward companies in that vertical
- For feature/product pages: show the most prestigious logos regardless of industry
- No copy needed here — none

---

## FOLD 3 — FEATURES INTRO

### What it needs to do
Bridge the gap between "I trust this company" (trust strip) and "let me see the details" (feature blocks). One centered block that names the capability area and gives the reader a reason to keep scrolling.

This is the section shown in the abstract management page screenshot — a centered heading + short subheading that sits above all the feature zig-zags.

### Structure

```
Heading (H2, centered): [Capability area + outcome for the reader]
    Max 12 words. Can be a statement or a soft promise.
    Example: "Easily Manage Abstract Submissions, Reviews & Decisions For Your Events"
    Rule: More specific than the H1 — this zooms into the capability set, the H1 was the benefit

Subheading (centered, 1–2 sentences): [What the reader can now do / what pain goes away]
    Max 35 words.
    Example: "Collect more quality submissions with blind, double-blind and peer reviews
             that help you select the best content for your events."
    Rule: Names the specific mechanism or approach — not just restates the heading
```

### What NOT to do
- Don't repeat the H1 verbatim — this should zoom in, not restate
- Don't stuff features here — that's what the blocks below are for
- Don't write more than 2 sentences — if you need more, it belongs in the feature blocks

---

## FOLD 4 — FEATURE BLOCKS

### What it needs to do
Prove that vFairs can actually do what the H1 promised. Each block = one specific job the reader needs done.

### Layout
Two-column: one side has the copy block, the other side has a screenshot or illustration. Alternate left/right between blocks (zig-zag).

### Block Structure (repeat 4–8 times)

```
H2: [Feature heading — plain English, conversational, specific]
    Style: Can be task-oriented ("Set up your speaker registration") OR
           outcome-oriented ("Every speaker briefed before event day")
    Rule: Plain language a non-marketer would say out loud.
          No jargon. No "leverage," "streamline," "empower."
          Max 6 words.

Single sentence: [One line that frames what this block covers]
    Rule: Directly states what the feature lets the reader do.
          Not a setup paragraph — one sentence only.
          Bad:  "Managing speakers across tools is a nightmare for event teams."
          Good: "Configure your full call for speakers without touching a spreadsheet."

Bullets: exactly 3–4 (hard max: 4)
    Rule: Each bullet = one specific thing the feature does, in plain English.
          Short. No sub-clauses. No jargon.
          Bad:  "Robust customizable submission form builder with conditional logic"
          Good: "Customize your form with questions and conditional logic"
          Bad:  "Automated multi-channel reviewer assignment workflows"
          Good: "Auto-assign reviewers by track or category"

[Optional] Block CTA: one text link or button
    Format: "[Action verb] + [specific thing]"
    Example: "Set up speaker registration" / "See how reviews work"
    Rule: Only include if there is a specific demo clip or relevant sub-page to link to.
          Do not add a generic "Learn More."

Visual: Screenshot or product illustration (right or left column)
    Note what the screenshot should show — be specific.
```

### H2 Style Examples

| Too long / jargony | Plain and specific |
|---|---|
| "Streamline Your Speaker Submission Workflow" | "Set up your speaker registration" |
| "Automated Reviewer Assignment and Workload Distribution" | "Assign reviewers without the back-and-forth" |
| "Comprehensive Speaker Profile Management Portal" | "Let speakers fill in their own profiles" |
| "Abstract-to-Session Conversion Automation" | "Turn accepted submissions into agenda sessions" |

### Bullet Examples

```
Bad:  "Customizable submission forms with conditional logic and dynamic field support"
Good: "Customize your form with questions and conditional logic to get the exact info you need"

Bad:  "Blind review mode with hidden submitter identity"
Good: "Hide submitter details from reviewers so evaluations stay objective"

Bad:  "Bulk CSV export of all speaker and session data"
Good: "Export all speaker data in one CSV whenever you need it"

Bad:  "Automated deadline reminder email communications"
Good: "Send deadline reminders automatically so speakers don't miss their window"
```

### Block Count by Page Type
- Feature page: 4–5 blocks
- Product page: 5–7 blocks
- Solution page: 6–8 blocks (covers the full event lifecycle)
- Industry page: 6–8 blocks (filtered to what this vertical cares about most)

## FOLD 5 — SOCIAL PROOF

### What it needs to do
Re-establish credibility mid-page before the reader considers leaving. Placed after feature sections.

### Structure

```
Featured quote:
  "[Specific outcome-focused customer quote — not generic praise]"
  — [Name], [Title], [Company]

Supporting stat or metric:
  "[Key number] — [what it means]"
  E.g. "1,758 verified G2 reviews — 4.7 out of 5"

[Optional] Case study callout:
  [Company Logo]
  [Key metric] — [outcome in 5 words]
  "Quote"
  → Read the Full Story
```

### Quote Quality Rules
- Specific beats general. "vFairs is great" = useless. "Badge scanning cut our check-in queue from 20 minutes to 3" = useful.
- Prefer quotes that name a specific feature or outcome this page covers
- Always include name, title, company — anonymous quotes feel fabricated

## FOLD 6 — USE CASE GRID

*(Solution and Industry pages only)*

### What it needs to do
Show range. Answer "does this work for MY type of event?"

### Structure

```
Heading: "Built for Every [Format / Audience Type] You Run"

Grid (6–8 cells):
| Event/Vertical Type | 1-sentence outcome-led description |
```

### Rules
- Each description = outcome for the organizer, not a feature list
- Bad: "Host medical conferences" → Good: "Run CME-accredited sessions with abstract management and CPD tracking"

## FOLD 9 — FAQ

### What it needs to do
Handle the top 4–6 objections or questions the reader has that the page didn't answer. Also valuable for PAA (People Also Ask) ranking and AI citations.

### Structure

```
Q: [Realistic question a real buyer would type into Google]
A: [Direct, specific answer — 2–4 sentences. Include nuance if needed.]
```

### FAQ Question Sources (in priority order)
1. Objections from buyer research ("is this just for large events?")
2. Integrations ("does it work with Salesforce / Marketo / Zoom?")
3. Setup and onboarding ("how long does it take to get started?")
4. Pricing ("how is this priced?")
5. Format/compatibility ("does this work for hybrid / in-person / virtual?")
6. Category-level education ("what is [feature/product]?") — only if KD research shows informational intent

### Rules
- Never write FAQ questions no actual buyer would ask
- Never write vague answers ("it depends on your needs")
- If an answer has a caveat or nuance — say it. That's where trust is built.
- Write for AI citation: a clear question + a direct, specific answer is how vFairs gets referenced in AI overviews

---

## FOLD 10 — CLOSING CTA

### What it needs to do
One last conversion shot. Re-state the value. Remove perceived risk.

### Structure

```
H2:   [Restate core outcome from H1 — different words]
Body: One sentence. Remove friction. "No commitment required. See [specific thing] live in 30 minutes."
CTA:  Book a Demo  |  Talk to an Expert
```

## Vocabulary Rules

### Always avoid

| Jargon word | Use instead |
|---|---|
| Seamless | Smooth / without friction / simple |
| Leverage | Use |
| Empower | Let / give / help |
| Facilitate | Run / make possible |
| Robust | Capable / detailed / full-featured |
| Holistic | Complete / end-to-end |
| Elevate | Improve |
| Transform | Change / rebuild |
| Intuitive | Easy to use / takes X minutes to learn |
| Best-in-class | "Top-rated on G2" / cite the actual award |
| Cutting-edge | [Describe what it actually does] |
| Streamline | [Say what gets faster or simpler — specifically] |
| World-class | [Cite Gartner / G2 recognition specifically] |

### Hard avoids (non-negotiable)
- Em dashes in body copy
- "It's not X, it's Y" contrasting structures
- AI-sounding dramatic imagery ("launch to a whisper, not a roar")
- Vague abstract claims without a specific scenario behind them
- Colons in H2 headings
- Exclamation points anywhere
- Passive voice in bullets ("results are delivered" → "you get results")

### Tone
Event professionals have been burned by tools that over-promised. Write as if you're a trusted colleague who knows the product deeply — confident, specific, direct, a little dry. Not a salesperson. Not a brochure.

## SEO Integration

| Element | Rule |
|---|---|
| H1 | Include primary keyword naturally — it usually appears if you name the feature/outcome correctly |
| H2s | Can include secondary and long-tail keywords — only if they read naturally |
| Body copy | Use ICP vocabulary from your research; don't stuff |
| FAQ questions | Mirror "People Also Ask" phrasing — write as someone would type it into Google |
| Meta title | `[Primary Keyword] \| vFairs` — under 60 characters |
| Meta description | Outcome + proof point + CTA — under 155 characters |

## Page Copy Checklist

Before finalizing any page, verify:

**Hero**
- [ ] Eyebrow = the primary SEO keyword, plain label, no verb or punctuation
- [ ] H1 = a value proposition (pain removal, platform advantage, or outcome-forward) — NOT the keyword repeated
- [ ] Subheading names 2–3 specific capabilities, passes "so what?" test, max 30 words
- [ ] CTAs are action + value, not generic ("Learn More" / "Get Started" = fail)

**Features Intro (Fold 3)**
- [ ] Centered heading zooms into the capability area — more specific than the H1
- [ ] Subheading names the mechanism or approach in 1–2 sentences
- [ ] Does NOT repeat the H1 or stuff feature details

**Feature Blocks (Fold 4)**
- [ ] Each H2 is plain English, max 6 words, conversational and specific
- [ ] Each block has exactly ONE setup sentence (not a paragraph)
- [ ] Each block has 3–4 bullets — hard max 4
- [ ] Each bullet names one specific thing in plain English — no sub-clauses, no jargon
- [ ] Block CTAs only included when there is a specific page or demo to link to

**Vocabulary (entire page)**
- [ ] No em dashes anywhere
- [ ] No banned words: seamless, leverage, empower, facilitate, robust, holistic, elevate, transform, intuitive, best-in-class, cutting-edge, world-class, streamline (unless extremely specific), "solution"
- [ ] No colons in H2 headings
- [ ] No exclamation points
- [ ] No passive voice in bullets

**FAQ**
- [ ] Questions are what a real buyer would type into Google
- [ ] Answers are 2–4 sentences, specific, no hedging
- [ ] At least one question targets an informational keyword from SEO research

**SEO**
- [ ] Primary keyword appears in eyebrow (not as the H1)
- [ ] Primary keyword appears naturally in meta title
- [ ] Secondary keywords appear in at least 2 H2s naturally
- [ ] Meta title under 60 characters
- [ ] Meta description under 155 characters
