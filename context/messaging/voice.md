# vFairs Voice & Tone

The non-negotiable rules for any copy, ad, email, landing page, or report generated by this repo for vFairs.

---

## Who we're talking to

Event professionals who have been burned by event tech that over-promised and under-delivered. They're operationally minded, busy, and immediately suspicious of marketing-speak. They've seen the brochures. They want proof.

**Buyer roles:**
- Director / VP of Marketing (B2B SaaS, associations, large enterprises)
- Director of Events / Event Operations
- Conference Producer
- Career Services / Talent Acquisition (for job fair use case)
- Membership / Community Director (for associations)

**What they hate in copy:**
- Vague promises ("seamless," "intuitive," "world-class")
- AI-generated cliches
- Vendor-speak that doesn't acknowledge their specific operational reality
- Anything that sounds like it was written by a chatbot

---

## Voice principles

| Principle | What it means | Don't |
|---|---|---|
| **Tactical** | Lead with specific outcomes, not abstract value | "Drive better engagement" |
| **Operational** | Use the language of event day, not marketing day | "Optimize attendee journeys" |
| **Confident but specific** | Claims are tied to proof (G2, Gartner, customer numbers) | "Best-in-class" with no source |
| **Direct** | Buyers are busy. Say it plainly | "Solutions that empower organizations to..." |
| **Plain English** | Non-native speakers should read smoothly | Idioms, compound jargon |

## Hard avoids (project-wide)

### Punctuation
- **No em dashes.** Use periods, colons, or restructure the sentence.
- Minimal exclamation points.
- No semicolons in body copy (OK in code).

### Sentence structures
- **No "It's not X, it's Y"** contrasting structures. Overused, sounds robotic.
- No rhetorical questions in H1s ("Tired of [pain]?").
- No "Imagine if..." openers.
- **No triple negation.** Never stack three "No X. No Y. No Z." sentences in a row — reads as an AI tell. Two is the absolute ceiling.

### Words and phrases
| Avoid | Why | Use instead |
|---|---|---|
| "Enterprise-grade" | Meaningless | Specific feature: "SOC 2 Type II, single sign-on, dedicated environment" |
| "Robust" | Vague | What specifically it does |
| "Seamless" | Vague + overused | "Works in one click" or describe what it does |
| "Command center" | AI-cliche | "Dashboard" or specific feature name |
| "Game-changer" | Hype | Specific outcome |
| "Cutting-edge" | Hype | What it actually does |
| "Best-in-class" | Vague claim | Cite G2/Gartner |
| "World-class" | Vague | Cite recognition |
| "Intuitive" | Vague | "Takes 5 minutes to learn" |
| "Drive engagement" | Vague | Specific behaviors enabled |
| "Empower" | Marketing cliche | "Lets you" |
| "Leverage" | Marketing-speak | "Use" |
| "Solutions" | Empty | What the thing actually does |

### Tone
- No exclamation points in product copy
- No emoji in vFairs marketing copy
- No corporate-speak ("We are excited to announce...")
- No "Imagine..." or "What if..." openers

### AI-sounding patterns
- "Ship features to a whisper, not a roar"
- "Don't just X, Y"
- "From [generic noun] to [aspirational noun]"
- Triplet sentence structures ("Build. Launch. Scale.")
- Heavy use of dashes for emphasis (the AI tell)

## Product accuracy (must-haves)

**vFairs check-in modalities:**
- QR codes
- RFID smart badges
- AI facial recognition

**Not NFC.** Never write NFC into product copy.

**Event types vFairs supports:**
- Virtual events (job fairs, career fairs, trade shows, conferences, networking)
- Hybrid events (combining in-person + virtual)
- In-person events (with mobile app + check-in)

**What vFairs is NOT:**
- A pure registration platform (we do more than that)
- A pure ticketing platform (we're not Eventbrite)
- A pure webinar tool (we're not Zoom Webinars)
- A community platform (we're not Bevy or Hivebrite)

**Lead enrichment:**
When an exhibitor scans an attendee's badge at a vFairs event, the platform triggers third-party enrichment (industry, company URL, company size, etc.) to produce a richer lead profile for the exhibitor's CRM. Safe to message on lead-capture and networking pages.

## Patterns to use

### Outcome-led H1s
**Bad:** "All-in-one virtual event platform for modern events"
**Good:** "Where sponsors actually get leads from your virtual exhibit hall"

### Feature-Capability-Benefit (FCB) bullets
**Bad:** "Real-time analytics dashboard"
**Good:** "See sponsor leads and engagement scores as the event runs, so you can intervene before exhibitors complain"

### Proof-led credibility
**Bad:** "Trusted by industry leaders"
**Good:** "1,800+ customers including Capgemini, Stryker, Hyundai, and the UN"

### Specific failure scenarios
**Bad:** "Don't let your event fall flat"
**Good:** "Your speaker no-shows 10 minutes before their session. Here's how vFairs handles it."

### H1 → H2 rules (landing pages)
- **H2 must take a different angle from H1, not paraphrase it.** If H1 makes a benefit promise, H2 should introduce proof, audience, or a specific capability. Never restate H1 in different words.
- **The H2 sub-head must EXPAND the H2 point.** It should never open with "A [category] is software that…" or any definitional template. Move directly into specifics, outcomes, or the buyer's situation.

## Workflow conventions

These are operational defaults for any skill that produces editable artifacts (landing-page rewrites, ad copy rewrites, mockups).

### Landing-page mockups
- The default mockup file is `[slug]-new.html` using the branded TT Norms Pro + coral-to-orange gradient kit (see `context/product/website-style-guide.md`).
- Hero form headings should be page-contextual. Never default to a generic "Capture Leads" on a feature page — match the buyer's intent (e.g. "Get a Demo of Badge Printing").
- Use `[Image #N]` placeholders for staged visuals; the team replaces them with approved assets before publish.
- Weave 2–3 Ahrefs semantic keywords into 2+ placements each (headings + body), surfaced by `/re-write`.

### Rewrite reviews
- **Never write rewrite updates back into the tab the user has been reviewing.** Always create a new tab in the destination Sheet. This preserves the reviewer's annotations and lets them diff old vs new side-by-side.
